Remain to complet the "Scanner Control System" a sort of remote control of the machine, that i start to develop. I's a system that give the possibility to control the differnts part of the system. Separately.

This box contains: One ATX power supply that i use for :

1 - feed the system of light LED. I use 2 electrical swich for power on separately the FRONT LED LIGHT - used for lighting the object to scann- and the BACK LED LIGHT - used for lighting the back ground of the box

2- I also need 12V power for the little 5 inc monitor, that give me the possibility to visualise the video out signal of the camera.

The more bigger problems that I have met are :


1- The plywood 5mm structure of the box is not sufficiently resistant : the nature of the structure, cylindrical with vertical uppercuts, it is not proper for support the strengths and the tensions produced by the weight of the rail and the camera support. Also, the tensions produced by the motor system of traction used for the muve the camera in the rail, make that the structure flex on itself.If I had not brought correction changes whit a C wood structure, is would be collapsed on it self.




2- The alluminium curved rail that i bild by myself in the fablab is obviously few linear. This ago that the camera moves with same attrition in the rail. This problem is relatively secondary, seen that the movement of the camera in the rail doesn't necessarily be fluid. The attrition however incrase the tensions, that added to the fragil nature of the wood structure surely increases the unstable movimentation of th camera support in the rail





The problems related to the structure have same easy solution: I have not decided yet which is the best new design solution for the structure, but probably the vertical uppercuts of the next prototype will be 8mm thread bar. Or always in plywood, but milled from 1.2mm hard wood. The metallic 8mm thread bar, will give me the possiblità to climb on and to get off more easily the structure and probably to make it more rigid, to damage of an increase of the weight of the scanner.morover i think to integrate the temporary solution used for rigidify the structure in the new prototype and use it like a transport handle.



for what it concerns the bending of the rail, It exist different and expensive solutions in commerce. From the system Igus to the water cutter of an aluminum profile.

Maybe another and more DIY solution could be to abandon the idea of a concave alluminium rail, that also works like guide of the wheels and reverse the concept: use some plexiglass of 8mm to create the rail and modify the wheels with un external supports and use it like a rail guide
